# Travellers

## About
You are a legendary hero who is well known across your town for your countless victories. While you're away on a well-deserved holiday, you receive ​an urgent call that your town is in danger.
You race back home to face the mobs threatening everyone and everything you fought so long to protect - will you be the hero your town needs?

## How to play
Move using the arrow keys. The rest of the combat is explained within the game itself.

## My progress
This was my first godot project involving player movement. This allowed me to learn and experiment with a lot of features I had never heard of before, such as using a camera2D, creating a character sprites and the actual code behind player movement.

As someone new to this style of game development, I found the project challenging at times. To manage this, I broke development down into smaller steps: First, I designed a rough idea of the different scenes on a whiteboard, and I referenced this all thoughout the process of the game. I then worked on creating the scripts for the diaglogue, and figuring out how to make the player move. I faced one challenge at a time, starting with the ones that were most familar to me, such as designing the tilemap, and then built upon that added new features I wasnt previously familar with, in this example, that included adding physics to each tile to prevent the player from moving off the map.

I also remained mindful of previous mistakes I had made in other games, such as ensuring my audio files were small to prevent a runtime error on Itch.io.

My favourite element of the game is probably the fight scenes, as these were heavily inspired by Omori and Pokemon. I also like how the mobs next move is random but dependent on it's health (e.g. if te mobs health is low, its more likely to peform a random rest move than a random skill move).

I enjoyed this project and I learnt a lot about top-down adventure games.
